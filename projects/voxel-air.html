Voxel Air

A 3D voxel editor you control with your hands in front of your webcam. It runs entirely in the browser — no install, no backend.

Live · runs in this browser

Raise a hand at your webcam and move a voxel — right here.

Loads the real app only when you ask. Prefer a new tab? Open live ↗

The problem

3D modelling tools assume a mouse and a manual. This one assumes hands.

What I built

A browser-native 3D voxel editor: you model by moving your hands in front of the webcam, and the whole thing ships as static files with zero backend — free to run and private by default.

The hardest decision

Doing all hand-tracking client-side with MediaPipe rather than sending frames to a server. It kept the app free to run, private by default, and deployable as static files — at the cost of making gesture detection stay stable on whatever webcam a stranger happens to have. That constraint drove most of the engineering.

The outcome

Deployed and in use. Getting feedback from developers internationally through GitHub — which is also where the gesture-stability edge cases keep coming from.
